Publications

Marine Pollution Bulletin · 2024

Evaluating the effects of inorganic UV filter titanium dioxide nanoparticles (nano-TiO₂) on early life stages of scleractinian coral Acropora tumida

Inorganic UV filters such as nano-TiO₂ are increasingly detected in marine environments, yet their effects on coral early life stages remain poorly characterised. Using the branching coral Acropora tumida, this study examined impacts on fertilisation success, embryonic development, and larval survival. While concentrations of 0.01–10 mg/L did not significantly affect fertilisation or embryonic development, exposure to 5 and 10 mg/L significantly reduced larval survival after four days. Current environmental TiO₂ concentrations are unlikely to pose acute threats to coral reproduction, though effects at higher concentrations warrant continued monitoring.

B.C.T. Cheung, J.C.H. Leong, E.Y.Y. Chan, T.K.T. Chang, A.S.U. Lau, C.G.W. Lee, E.L.C. Wong, I.W.Y. Tse, L.D. Liu, M.H. Kwok, M.H.C. Chan, T. Ngai, A.P.Y. Chui

doi.org/10.1016/j.marpolbul.2024.117231
