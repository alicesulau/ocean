Alice Lau

Marine biologist & conservation scientist

From subtropical coral gardens to seagrass meadows, the ocean has always compelled me to ask why things are the way they are — and what it would take to keep them that way. My research spans coral community structure, octocoral reproduction, and the environmental drivers of coral decline and restoration. I am equally drawn to the broader subtidal world: the ecology of seaweed and seagrass, the services these systems provide, and the emerging possibilities of marine bioeconomy.
